Ecosystems

There are many ecosystems within the MDB, which are home to both native and endangered species. While many of these ecosystems are quite resilient, some plant life are unable to weather the unnatural and extended drought which then lead to the destruction of the habitats of our native and endangered species.

The vegetation throughout the MDB plays a significant role in keeping balance within the ecosystem. The wetland and floodplain vegetation is able to filter harmful chemicals out of the river system and keep the soil healthy.

The trees and plants along the riverbanks also prevent erosion, assist in the natural flow of water and store carbon dioxide.

Although with extended droughts or unnatural chemicals being introduced into the rivers, these plant can die out. Along with them goes the stability they provided to the ecosystem and without the ecosystems foundation it dies out.

The wildlife that call these ecosystems home, suffer greatly when the ecosystems are thrown off balance. With many of the animals in the ecosystem being native to these areas of Australia or being endangered protecting them and their environment is a priority.

The list includes, 367 bird species (35 of which are endangered), including 98 species of waterbirds, 85 mammal species (20 of which are extinct, 16 endangered) over 50 native fish species, 31 native frogs species, 46 snake species (5 endangered), 100 lizard species (1 endangered), 3 freshwater turtle species and 124 families of macroinvertebrates.

About The MDBA

We are responsible for directing the sharing of the River Murray's water on behalf of the Basin states. The Murray–Darling Agreement, (a schedule of the Water Act 2007) spells out these arrangements.

The sustainable management of the Murray–Darling Basin river system is a collective endeavour of the Australian and Basin state governments, with river dependent industries and communities.

We acknowledge the traditional owners and custodians of Country throughout the Murray–Darling Basin and acknowledge their continuing connection to land, waters and community. We pay our respects to the people, the cultures and the Elders past, present and emerging.

How To Help

Southern Basin Local Farmers

Southern Basin Local farmers is a group of farmers in the Southern Basin who have been fighting for the water they should be getting. Help them continue to support their communities by donating today so they are able to give take power back from large corporations and give it to smaller community farmers.

Indigenous Community Support Group

Many indigenous communities have been hit heavily by the deterioration of the Murray Darling Basin. This is due to the value that their communities place on the land, their lifestyles have been changed due to not being able to live off the rivers. So help us support these communities to regain their way of life and have more control over how the water on their land is used.

Murray Darling Wildlife

After the damage dealt to the ecosystems around the Murray Darling Basin during the past 7 years, there has finally been floods which broke the drought in many Southern Basin areas. Although this is the perfect opportunity to relocate animals to better thriving areas, we also need help in repairing areas that have been eroded, wasted away or dried out.
